🦐Nutrition & Health Benefits

  • High Protein: A standard 85-gram serving offers roughly 18 grams of clean protein for only 85 calories.
  • Heart Health: They are packed with omega-3 fatty acids, which help lower triglycerides and reduce your risk of heart disease.
  • Vitamins: They are an excellent source of vitamin B12 for energy, as well as essential minerals like selenium and zinc.
  • Diet Friendly: They have virtually zero carbohydrates, making them ideal for blood sugar management.

🦐Cooking & Preparation

  1. Thawing: If frozen, thaw the shrimp in a bowl of cold water for about 15–20 minutes. Avoid using warm water, as this can affect the texture.
  2. Peeling: Decide whether to leave the tails on for presentation. To peel, pull off the legs and then use your thumb to slide under the shell and lift it away from the meat.
  3. Deveining: Use a small paring knife to make a shallow slit along the back of the shrimp. Use the tip of the knife or a toothpick to lift out the dark vein (the digestive tract) and rinse under cold water.
  4. Drying: Pat the shrimp completely dry with paper towels. This ensures they sear properly rather than steaming in the pan.
